Sections 211-215 and 464 of the Code of Criminal Procedure:
Section 211: Specifies the contents of the charge.
Section 212: Requires the charge to contain particulars of the time, place, and person involved.
Section 215: Errors in the charge are not material unless they mislead the accused or result in a failure of justice.
Section 464: Provides that errors or omissions in the charge do not invalidate the trial unless they result in a failure of justice.
Judicial Precedents:
Willie (William) Slaney v. State of Madhya Pradesh (1956): Emphasizes the importance of ensuring that an accused person gets a fair trial.
Gurbachan Singh v. State of Punjab (1957): Courts must look to the substance and not technicalities.
Shamnsaheb M. Multtani v. State of Karnataka (2001): Convictions are valid if there is no failure of justice despite omissions or irregularities in the charge.
