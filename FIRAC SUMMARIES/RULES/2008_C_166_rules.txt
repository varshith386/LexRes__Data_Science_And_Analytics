Section 21 of the M.P. Uchacha Nyayalay (Khand Nyaypeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005:

Provides for appeals from judgments or orders of a single judge in exercise of original jurisdiction under Article 226, but not under Article 227.
Article 226 and Article 227 of the Constitution of India:

Article 226: Original jurisdiction to issue writs.
Article 227: Supervisory jurisdiction over subordinate courts and tribunals.
Judicial Precedents:

Hari Vishnu Kamath v. Syed Ahmad Ishaque (1955): Distinguishes powers under Articles 226 and 227.
Umaji Keshao Meshram v. Radhikabai (1986): Clarifies that proceedings under Article 226 are original civil proceedings.
Sushilabai Laxminarayan Mudliyar v. Nihalchand Waghajibhai Shaha (1993): Differentiates between the principal relief sought under Article 226 and ancillary directions under Article 227.
Surya Dev Rai v. Ram Chander Rai (2003): Discusses the overlap and distinction between Articles 226 and 227.
