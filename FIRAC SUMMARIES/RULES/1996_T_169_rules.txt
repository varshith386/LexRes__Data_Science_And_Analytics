Section 46(1)(b) of the Trade Marks Act allows for removal of a registered trademark due to non-use for five consecutive years.
Section 46(3) provides exceptions for non-use due to special circumstances in the trade.
Section 48(2) deems permitted use of a trademark as use by the proprietor for the purpose of rectification.
Discretionary power under Section 46 to consider public and commercial interests when deciding on rectification applications.
