Unexplained Delay: According to established jurisprudence, delay in passing a detention order must be satisfactorily explained to maintain the validity of the order.
Non-Placement of Vital Documents: All relevant and material documents must be placed before the detaining authority to ensure a fair decision-making process.
Non-Supply of Documents: The detenue must be supplied with all relevant and material documents to enable effective representation, as per Article 22(5) of the Constitution of India.
Non-Application of Mind: The detaining authority must demonstrate a genuine and real application of mind to the materials before it.
