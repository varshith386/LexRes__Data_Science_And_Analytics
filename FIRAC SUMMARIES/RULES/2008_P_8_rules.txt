Section 46 of the Trade Marks Act: Pertains to the removal of a trademark from the register due to non-use for a continuous period of five years and one month.
Section 56 of the Trade Marks Act: Provides a broader ground for rectification, allowing any aggrieved person to apply for cancellation, expunging, or varying an entry in the register.
Case Law: Various precedents highlight the interpretation of "for the time being" and the requirements for bona fide use of a trademark, including the need to consider the non-use period in context and the rights conferred by registered trademarks.
