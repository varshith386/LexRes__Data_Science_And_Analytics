Section 302 IPC: Pertains to the punishment for murder.
Section 392 read with Section 397 IPC: Pertains to robbery and the use of deadly weapons.
Section 27 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872: Allows the admissibility of information leading to the discovery of a fact.
Case Laws on Circumstantial Evidence:
Hukam Singh v. State of Rajasthan (1977): Inferences of guilt must be incompatible with the innocence of the accused.
Hanumant Govind Nargundkar v. State of Madhya Pradesh (1952): Circumstances must exclude every hypothesis except the guilt of the accused.
Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra (1984): Conditions for conviction based on circumstantial evidence.
