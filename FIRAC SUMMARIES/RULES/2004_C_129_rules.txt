Promissory Estoppel and Legitimate Expectation: These doctrines apply when a promise or assurance by the government induces action or forbearance on the part of the promisee to their detriment. The promise or assurance must be clear, reasonable, and intended to affect legal relations.
Natural Justice: Fairness and adherence to principles of natural justice are mandatory when administrative actions affect rights or benefits of individuals, including the right to be heard before adverse decisions.
Retrospective Legislation: Generally disfavored, retrospective legislation is permissible only if expressly authorized by law or in cases of public interest.
