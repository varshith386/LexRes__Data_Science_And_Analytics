Natural Justice: Principles of natural justice, including the right to be heard (audi alteram partem), must be observed in quasi-judicial functions and reviews.
Judicial Precedents:
Vrajlal Manilal & Co. v. Union of India: Government must observe natural justice in quasi-judicial actions.
Shivaji Nathubhai v. Union of India: Government must act judicially under certain review rules.
Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India: Necessity of a post-decisional hearing if a pre-decisional hearing is impractical.
Swadeshi Cotton Mills v. Union of India: Full post-decisional hearing following urgent actions without pre-decisional hearing.
Liberty Oil Mills v. Union of India: Natural justice principles should not be excluded unless explicitly mandated.
Grounds for Challenging Subordinate Legislation: Including non-compliance with stipulated considerations, arbitrariness, and constitutional or statutory violations.
