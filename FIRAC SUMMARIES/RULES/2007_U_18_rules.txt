Circumstantial Evidence: The prosecution must establish a complete chain of evidence pointing unerringly to the guilt of the accused (Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra).
Burden of Proof: In cases relying on circumstantial evidence, the prosecution must prove its case beyond a reasonable doubt, and any gap in the chain can lead to acquittal (Mahmood v. State of U.P.).
Section 376 IPC: Conviction under this section requires proof that intercourse occurred without consent.
Section 309 IPC: For an attempted suicide charge, there must be evidence showing intent and action towards committing suicide.
