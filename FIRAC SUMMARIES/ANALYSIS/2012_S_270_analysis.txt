The prosecution established that Pathan Haseena Begum died due to hanging and provided evidence of persistent demands for dowry and harassment.
Witnesses corroborated incidents of harassment and non-payment of dowry, leading to cruelty inflicted on the deceased.
Medical evidence supported death by hanging, consistent with the prosecution's narrative of harassment and pressure for dowry.
The courts applied the legal presumption under Section 113B of the Evidence Act due to harassment for dowry shortly before her death.
